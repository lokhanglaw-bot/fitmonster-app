# Research Notes: Caring Mechanics for FitMonster

## Key Findings from Research

### Finch App (Self-Care + Virtual Pet)
- Birb companion grows as user completes self-care tasks
- Progressive discovery: features unlock gradually to build investment
- Micro-interactions: head tilts, blinks, idle animations make pet feel alive
- Haptic feedback mimics gentle physical contact when petting
- Investment loop: more time spent → more invested in pet's growth → more invested in own self-care
- Community features and social proof

### Capy Diet (Calorie Tracking + Virtual Pet)
- Virtual pet Capybara grows and evolves with weight loss progress
- Pet serves as coach and buddy
- Tracks calorie deficit
- Personalized meal plans and recipes
- Pet appearance reflects user's progress
- Rewards and unlockable features for engagement

### Yu-Kai Chou's Pet Companion Design (Gamification Framework)
- Health of virtual pet directly correlates with user's physical activity
- If user reaches daily goals → pet appears happy and healthy
- If user is inactive → pet appears sad or unwell
- Taps into user's emotions and sense of responsibility
- Users aren't just exercising for themselves; they're exercising for their pet
- Pet evolution mirrors user's progress

### Vi Pet! Go! & TameWatch
- Step tracking linked to pet health
- Pet responds to ALL healthy activities
- Tamagotchi-style care mechanics combined with fitness

### Key Design Principles
1. **Emotional Connection** - Pet must feel alive and responsive
2. **Direct Correlation** - User behavior directly affects pet state
3. **Positive Reinforcement** - Celebrate achievements, don't just punish neglect
4. **Progressive Discovery** - Unlock features gradually
5. **Loss Aversion** - Fear of pet declining motivates action (but use sparingly)
6. **Social Proof** - Show others' pets to motivate
